# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pollyana's Dolce team members who use the ERP during daily bakery and pastry operations: production, sales, inventory, purchasing, logistics, administration, and management. The interface should be Spanish-first for real operator language, and clear enough to help the team complete work quickly with less confusion and less dependency on paper, spreadsheets, or generic enterprise software patterns.

The same brand context also supports Pollyana's Dolce public website and online store, where customers should understand the quality of the bakery, feel confidence in the business, and have a smoother purchase or inquiry experience than a typical small-business site.

## Product Purpose

The ERP exists to let Pollyana's Dolce run its own operations through a purpose-built interface instead of adapting bakery workflows to generic systems like Oracle or SAP. Success means the team can capture, review, and act on operational information with fewer steps, fewer mistakes, and a better visual experience.

For public-facing surfaces, success means the website, store, and marketing pages make Pollyana's Dolce feel polished, trustworthy, appetizing, and professionally run without losing the warmth of a bakery brand.

## Brand Personality

Polished, warm, capable.

The product should feel practical and serious enough for business operations, but not cold, corporate, or generic. It should carry the care, detail, and quality of a bakery and pastry shop into the UI: refined, inviting, and easy to understand.

## Anti-references

Do not make the ERP feel like a default enterprise suite: avoid dense Oracle/SAP-style screens, generic admin templates, lifeless gray dashboards, cramped tables, and workflows that look technically complete but feel unpleasant to use.

Do not make the public site feel like a generic bakery template, stock-photo storefront, or small-business brochure page. Avoid design choices that look mass-produced, overly corporate, or disconnected from the actual Pollyana's Dolce product and operations.

## Design Principles

1. Make daily work feel lighter: every ERP screen should reduce friction for the person doing the task, not expose database structure.
2. Carry brand quality into operations: internal tools should look as considered as customer-facing pages.
3. Be clear before being decorative: visual polish should support scanning, decisions, and error prevention.
4. Preserve source-of-truth boundaries: Point owns catalog/product/insumos truth, RRHH owns people, auth owns credentials, and the ERP should validate, replicate, guard, and explain those states rather than creating duplicate registries.
5. Prefer bakery-specific workflows over generic enterprise patterns: the interface should reflect production, sales, inventory, branches, logistics, maintenance, bonuses, and team routines.
6. Build trust through precision: totals, statuses, actions, confirmations, live production visibility, and export states should be visually obvious and hard to misread.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, focus states, and readable typography. Support reduced-motion preferences. Avoid relying on color alone for operational status, approvals, warnings, or errors. Interfaces should remain usable during long work sessions, on common laptop screens, and on mobile when workflows require it.
